Cold Brew Coffee

Description:

Don't confuse cold-brew coffee with iced coffee. Although both are coffee, cold brew's slower, room-temperature brewing method helps the coffee develop a less bitter taste. This wonderful recipe makes it worth the time it takes to brew!

This recipe is taken from Love and Lemons's blog. Give her a visit, her recipes are so good!!!

Ingredients:

  • 1.5 cups fresh coarsely ground coffee
  • 3 cups filtered water
  • Milk, can be regular or plant milk

    • Steps:

    1. Place the ground coffee in a 28 oz jar. Add water and stir. Cover and set adie for 12-24 hours at room temperature.
    2. Spread a piece of cheesecloth in a fine mesh strainer over a large bowl. Pour the coffee into the bowl to strain out the ground coffee. Store the strained coffee in the fridge.
    3. When ready to serve, pour over ice and add milk as desired.
    4. If the coffee is stronger than you'd like ,dilute it with water or milk.
